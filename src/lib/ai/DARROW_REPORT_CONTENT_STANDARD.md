## THE ONE-SENTENCE STANDARD

**The client must close the report thinking: "This explains me."**

Not: "Interesting, but vague."
Not: "I could have read this anywhere."
Not: "This is a technical summary of my chart."

But: "This is my architecture. I recognize it."

---

## WHAT A DARROW CODE REPORT IS

A premium personal astrology report that translates astrological, numerological,
and Bazi data into **lived experience** — the specific way one person processes
pressure, makes decisions, manages energy, and moves through time.

It is not:

- A technical chart reading
- A generic horoscope
- A motivational document
- A list of personality traits
- A promise of outcomes

It is:

- A structural mirror
- An orientation tool
- A decision-making reference
- A stabilizing document the client returns to

## CONTENT QUALITY CHECKLIST

Every section must pass all of these:

### ✅ Scenario test

Does the section open with or include a concrete scenario the client
would recognize from their own life? Not abstract. Not a metaphor. An actual scene.
— "You are mid-conversation. Something small shifts in tone..."
— "You are offered a contract. Numbers align. But inside — silence."

### ✅ Mechanism test

Does the section explain HOW the pattern works, not just that it exists?
— Not: "You are sensitive to your environment."
— But: "Your Water dominant system absorbs atmospheric data the way other
people absorb noise. By the time someone has finished speaking, your
internal scanner has already assessed: safe / unsafe, real / performed."

### ✅ Specificity test

Is every claim anchored to a named placement, aspect, element, or number
that actually exists in this person's chart data?
— Not: "Your emotions run deep."
— But: "Sun conjunct Moon in Cancer (0°23') means your conscious identity
and subconscious processing are fused. You do not observe your emotional
read of a situation and then decide — you are the read."

### ✅ Protocol test

Does the section include at least one PROTOCOL or Warning Signal where
the spec requires it? Is the protocol concrete and behavioral?
— Not: "Try to be more patient."
— But: "PROTOCOL: Before committing to any significant decision, identify
whether the impulse arrives from a clear interior signal or from external
pressure. If the decision feels urgent, wait one sleep cycle. If the
urgency is gone in the morning, the decision was pressure-driven."

### ✅ Voice test (Dinner Table Test)

Can every sentence be said to an intelligent adult friend in a relaxed
conversation? If not — rewrite or cut.
— Fails: "Your soul mission is to activate your inner abundance frequency."
— Passes: "Your income mechanism is not labor exchange. It is leverage —
the monetization of insight, positioning, and accumulated depth."

### ✅ Tone test

Does the section hold the 70% structural / 30% warm balance?
— Not cold technical: "Sun conjunct Moon 0°23'. See Moon in Cancer."
— Not warm sloganeering: "You are a beacon of light for those around you!"
— But: "The system runs on depth, not speed. This is not a weakness.
It is a configuration that processes at a different frequency than
most — and what it produces, when given proper operating conditions,
is genuinely uncommon."

| Outcome guarantees      | "This will bring wealth" / "You will find love when..."                  |

| Directives              | "You must..." / "You need to..." / "You should..."                       |

## STRUCTURAL REQUIREMENTS BY MODULE

### CORE (18–20 pages / 3,000–3,600 words / 17 sections)

Primary purpose: "Who am I — structurally?"
Each section = one structural layer (see darrowcode_core_module_spec.md).
Must include: scenario, mechanism, protocol, proof tags per section.
Must close with: executive summary + before/after + soft bridge.

### ADD-ONS (8–10 pages / 1,200–1,500 words / 10 sections)

Primary purpose: "How does this structure play out in [domain]?"
Each add-on zooms into one life domain using the same underlying architecture.
Must include: module_opening, primary_architecture, mechanism, key_pattern,
timing, protocols, shadow, before_after, summary, bridge.
(See darrowcode_addon_modules_spec.md for domain-specific requirements.)

### FULL CODE / CORE COMPLETE (65–75 pages / 12,000–14,000 words)

All modules + grand_synthesis.
grand_synthesis = 400–500 words, meta-level convergence across all modules.
Must name: the central theme that all modules confirmed, the primary
operating asset, the primary friction point, the most important timing signal.

## THE BEFORE / AFTER STANDARD

Every module must include a Before/After block.
This is NOT a promise of results. It is a shift in experience.

Canonical form:
"Before: [specific inner experience without the map]
After: [same reality, now with structural context]"

Examples that pass:

- "Before: The depth of processing feels like slowness — a liability.
  After: The depth is the instrument. It takes time because it reads
  more layers than a faster system would."
- "Before: The gap between wanting and acting feels like fear.
  After: The gap is the diesel warm-up. The power is already there."

Examples that fail:

- "Before: You struggled. After: Now you will thrive." ← outcome promise
- "Before: You were confused. After: Everything is clear." ← too generic

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY STANDARD

Every CORE report must close with an Executive Summary containing exactly:

1. **Core Advantage** — 1–2 sentences: the primary structural gift
2. **Primary Sensitivity** — 1–2 sentences: the primary vulnerability (no shame)
3. **Decision Formula** — 1–2 sentences: this person's specific decision key
4. **Core Conclusion** — 1 sentence: the whole architecture in one line
5. **Timing Note** — current year/cycle and what it means for now
6. **Soft bridge** — one reference to what comes next, no sales pressure

## PROOF TAGS FORMAT

Proof tags anchor every section to real data.
Place at the end of the section prose, in brackets:
[Sun conjunct Moon Cancer 0°23' · Water Dominant 59% · Life Path 3 · DM Gui Water]

Rules:

- Only reference placements/aspects/numbers that exist in the received data
- Do not invent or estimate placements
- If birth time is unknown, do not include Ascendant or house tags
- Keep to 3–5 tags per section

## 2 · POLICY RULES (active now)

### 2.1 These are symbolic anchors, not lucky charms

In all Darrow Code output:

- Colors are **psychological anchors** — they support a pattern, they do not
  magically create outcomes
- Stones are **symbolic mirrors** — they reflect a structural quality, they do
  not protect, heal, or attract
- Symbolic allies are **resonance markers** — they represent a quality, they
  do not have literal power

### 2.2 Required explanation format

Every indicator must be explained. Never output a bare label.

✅ Correct:
"Supportive color: deep blue — as a psychological anchor for emotional
coherence, quiet authority, and the depth of focus your architecture is
built around."

❌ Wrong:
"Lucky color: blue"
"Your stone is amethyst."
"Color: indigo"

### 2.3 No claim types allowed

- No "brings luck" claims
- No "heals" claims
- No "protects from" claims
- No "attracts" claims
- No "manifests" claims
- No medical claims
- No financial claims

### 2.4 Data grounding requirement

Each color/stone/ally assignment must be:
- derived from the client's natal data, element balance, or numerology profile
- explained in terms of that specific pattern
- NOT assigned randomly or generically from a generic sun-sign list

### 2.5 Curated dictionary requirement

Before any color, stone, or symbolic ally is generated in production:
- A specific mapping rule must exist in this file
- The rule must be original Darrow Code writing
- The rule must not copy protected correspondence tables
- The rule must pass the Dinner Table Test and the no-claims policy

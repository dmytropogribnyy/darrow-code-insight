## 0. Core rule

Gemstones may be used in Darrow Code only as **symbolic / psychological / aesthetic mirrors**, not as remedial tools.

Allowed:

- gemstone names as cultural / symbolic motifs;
- physical color / material notes from gemological sources;
- broad historical / cultural lore when softly framed;
- source-log notes about monthly birthstones, zodiac birthstones, planetary stones, and Navaratna/Vedic traditions;
- original Darrow Code interpretation language;
- future STYLE or identity-card symbolic accents only after curated-dictionary approval.

Not allowed:

- medical, healing, fertility, mental-health, blood/circulation, detox, addiction, or disease claims;
- wealth, luck, success, fame, marriage, pregnancy, protection, curse-removal, energy-cleansing, or guaranteed attraction claims;
- gemstone calculator recommendations based on kundali/birth chart as product advice;
- instructions for which finger, metal, carat/ratti weight, ritual timing, mantra, or body placement;
- telling users they should buy/wear a gemstone;
- copying gemstone descriptions, retailer text, charts, images, palettes, or proprietary lists wholesale;
- presenting astrology-gemstone traditions as scientifically proven.

Darrow phrasing rule:

```text
Use “symbolic anchor”, “visual mirror”, “aesthetic cue”, “psychological reminder”, or “poetic accent”.
Do not use “remedy”, “cure”, “activates”, “protects”, “attracts”, “guarantees”, “balances your energy”, or “fixes your chart”.
```

## 2. Key finding: there is no single gemstone system

The reviewed sources point to several different systems that overlap but do not fully agree:

1. **Modern monthly birthstones** — common jewellery-industry list, e.g. January/Garnet, February/Amethyst, March/Aquamarine, April/Diamond, May/Emerald, June/Pearl/Alexandrite variants, July/Ruby, August/Peridot/Spinel, September/Sapphire, October/Tourmaline/Opal, November/Topaz/Citrine, December/Blue Topaz/Tanzanite/Turquoise variants.
2. **Traditional monthly birthstones** — older lists differ from modern lists.
3. **Tropical zodiac birthstones** — stones assigned by sign, e.g. Aries/Bloodstone or Diamond, Taurus/Sapphire or Emerald, etc.; lists vary by source.
4. **Planetary stones in Western astrology** — stones assigned by planet/luminary, e.g. Sun/Ruby, Moon/Pearl, Mercury/Emerald.
5. **Vedic/Navaratna gemstone traditions** — nine gems tied to Navagraha / planetary forces; often framed as remedial wearing.
6. **Retailer-specific alternatives** — many jewellery sites provide alternative stones for accessibility, aesthetics, or sales categories.

Darrow rule:

```text
Never present one gemstone as “the correct stone” for a person.
If gemstones are ever used, describe the selected stone as a symbolic mirror chosen from a curated Darrow dictionary.
```

## 3. Source-specific notes

### 3.1 DWS Jewellery — Western planetary stones

Useful concept:

- Planetary gemstone mapping as a market-recognizable taxonomy.
- Planet = symbolic function; gemstone = visual/material mirror.

Risk:

- The article uses strong metaphysical benefit language: empowerment, healing, prosperity, protection, spiritual benefit, luck, etc.
- It is a commercial jewellery source.

Safe Darrow use:

```text
Sun/Ruby may be logged as a symbolic association between solar visibility and red-gold vitality.
Do not say ruby boosts leadership, career success, blood, vitality, or charisma.
```

---

### 3.2 International Gem Society — monthly birthstone chart

Useful concept:

- Gemological source for monthly birthstone lists.
- Explains modern vs traditional birthstones and why lists changed.
- Useful for physical facts: gem family, colors, hardness/wearability, varieties.

Risk:

- Some lore sections mention protection, healing, spirituality, or old beliefs.

Safe Darrow use:

```text
Use IGS to verify that a gemstone is commonly associated with a birth month and to check physical/color facts.
Do not import lore as product claim.
```

---

### 3.3 NAJ — zodiac birthstone chart

Useful concept:

- Industry consumer-advice source with a clean month/sign/traditional/alternative chart.
- Good for comparing sign-based gemstone lists.

Risk:

- Contains healing/energy/mysticism framing.

Safe Darrow use:

```text
Use as source comparison only.
If NAJ says Aries/Diamond/Bloodstone, treat it as one tradition, not a universal truth.
```

---

### 3.4 GIA — authoritative gemological grounding

Useful concept:

- Authoritative gem education and gem encyclopedia.
- Best source among this set for gemstone facts and mainstream birthstone education.

Risk:

- GIA mentions myth/lore around birthstones, but Darrow must keep lore separated from fact.

Safe Darrow use:

```text
Use GIA for factual gemstone background and source-log grounding.
Do not turn gem lore into deterministic claims.
```

---

### 3.5 Gemsmantra — Vedic gemstone calculator

Useful concept:

- Shows how the remedial-gemstone market collects birth data and recommends stones/fingers.
- Useful for understanding what Darrow Code should avoid.

Risk:

- Requests personal/contact data and birth data.
- Gives wearing-finger recommendations.
- Strong remedial framing.

Darrow blocked:

```text
No gemstone calculator.
No finger/metal/weight/ritual recommendations.
No “ideal gemstone” output.
No customer PII collection for gemstone advice.
```

## 8. Forbidden claim library

- “This stone heals…”
- “This stone cures…”
- “This stone protects you from…”
- “This stone removes negative energy…”
- “This stone attracts love/money/success/luck…”

- “Use this carat/ratti weight…”
- “This gemstone balances your planet/chart/kundali…”

## 11. Source-log notes from this review

- DWS Jewellery provides a commercial Western planetary-stone taxonomy useful for market/category awareness, but includes metaphysical benefit claims that Darrow must not reproduce.
- IGS is a stronger reference for modern/traditional monthly birthstone lists and gemological facts; it still contains lore that must remain non-claiming.
- GIA is the strongest gemological reference in this set for mainstream birthstone education and gemstone facts.
- NAJ and Royal Coster are useful for comparing zodiac-birthstone tables and alternative stones, but both contain traditional healing/protection/luck meanings that must be filtered.
- Gemsmantra and GemAstro are useful mainly as high-risk examples of Vedic/remedial gemstone marketing and forbidden claims.
- AstrologicalGemstones.com and Cherry Tree Beads should remain pending manual review because current fetch was unavailable/incomplete.
